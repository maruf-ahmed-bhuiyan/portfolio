Nipah virus epidemiology in Bangladesh

At IEDCR I help characterize the epidemiological diversity of circulating Nipah strains. The virus causes seasonal, high-fatality encephalitis outbreaks concentrated in the country's central and north-western "Nipah belt," with recurrent spillover linked to the winter date-palm sap season.

Transmission is driven by consumption of raw date-palm sap contaminated by fruit-bat (Pteropus) excreta, followed by person-to-person spread through close contact and healthcare exposure. Prevention therefore centres on avoiding raw sap, heating or boiling sap before consumption, protecting collection vessels from bat access, and rigorous infection-prevention and contact precautions in care settings.
